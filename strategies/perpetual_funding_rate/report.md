## Alpha Story

Perpetual swap funding rates reflect the cost of holding leveraged directional
exposure. When the market is net-long and bullish, longs pay shorts (positive
funding); when net-short, shorts pay longs (negative funding). Extreme positive
funding signals overcrowded longs: carry cost erodes long P&L, eventually forcing
de-leveraging and mean-reversion. Extreme negative funding signals the reverse.
Who is on the other side: leveraged trend-followers and momentum traders paying
the funding bill; hedgers on the short side receiving it.
Why the edge should persist: funding reflects a real cash transfer every 8 hours,
so the signal is grounded in economics, not just price. Crowded positioning unwinds
mechanically when carry cost exceeds expected gain.
Expected value: contrarian mean-reversion after extreme funding spikes (|z| > 2),
with a holding horizon of 8-48 hours (1-6 funding intervals).
Expected decay horizon: 12-24 months; arb funds explicitly farm funding extremes,
compressing the edge as AUM grows.
