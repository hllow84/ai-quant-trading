## Alpha Story

The Coinbase premium gap is the spread between BTC/USD on Coinbase (US retail and
institutional spot) and BTC/USDT on Binance (global perpetual reference price).
Mechanism: when US-side buying pressure exceeds cross-venue arbitrage capacity,
Coinbase price rises above Binance. Arbitrageurs close the spread by transferring
BTC from Binance to Coinbase, which tends to coincide with, and slightly precede,
a short-term up-move in the global reference price.
Who is on the other side: statistical arbitrageurs and market-makers exploiting the
spread; retail traders on Coinbase paying a premium for immediacy.
Why the edge persists: on-chain BTC transfers take 10-60 minutes; stablecoin
liquidity on Coinbase is shallower than Binance, limiting instantaneous arb.
Expected value: mean-reversion of extreme spikes (|premium| > 2 sigma) within
2-6 hours on 1h bars.
Expected decay horizon: 6-18 months of active trading before arb infrastructure
compresses the edge materially.
